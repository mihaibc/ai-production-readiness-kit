# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

AI Production Readiness Kit is intended to be a practical, respectful space for people working on production AI readiness, governance, evals, observability, and delivery quality.

Everyone participating in this project is expected to communicate with professionalism, curiosity, and respect.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, examples, and maintainer interactions connected to this repository.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful of different backgrounds, experience levels, roles, and viewpoints.
- Give feedback that is specific, constructive, and focused on the work.
- Ask clarifying questions before assuming bad intent.
- Keep discussions relevant to the project and its goals.
- Respect privacy, confidentiality, and legal boundaries when sharing examples.
- Use synthetic examples when discussing sensitive or employer-specific workflows.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private, confidential, or identifying information without permission.
- Dismissing or belittling people based on background, seniority, employer, geography, or role.
- Repeated off-topic, disruptive, or bad-faith participation.
- Submitting examples that expose confidential customer, employer, personal, or regulated data.

## Reporting

If you see behavior that does not fit this code of conduct, contact the project maintainer through a private channel where possible. If a public issue or discussion contains sensitive material, avoid amplifying it and ask for it to be removed.

Useful reports include:

- what happened
- where it happened
- who was involved
- relevant links, screenshots, or timestamps
- whether confidential material needs to be removed

## Enforcement

The maintainer may remove content, close discussions, reject contributions, or block participants when behavior harms the project or its community.

The goal is not to police disagreement. The goal is to keep the project useful, safe, and professional.
